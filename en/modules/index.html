Fundamentals

The Staff, Clefs, and Notes

Learn the musical staff, treble and bass clefs, and the natural notes A through G. Place notes on the correct lines and spaces.

Basic Rhythmic Figures

Learn whole notes, half notes, quarter notes, and eighth notes. Understand their durations and how they fill a measure.

Simple Time Signatures

Master time signatures 4/4, 3/4, and 2/4. Compose melodies that fit each meter naturally.

Intervals — Unison to Octave

Learn all simple intervals from unison to octave. Identify and write major, minor, and perfect intervals.

Basic Harmony

#7

Major and Minor Triads

Build major and minor triads by stacking thirds. A major triad has a major third on the bottom and minor third on top. A minor triad is the reverse.

🎵 75 XP ▶ Play Module
#8

Triad Inversions

Learn root position, first inversion (3rd in bass), and second inversion (5th in bass). Inversions create smoother bass lines.

🎵 100 XP ▶ Play Module
#9

I-IV-V-I Progression

The most fundamental chord progression in Western music. The I chord is home, IV is the subdominant, V creates tension that resolves back to I.

🎵 100 XP ▶ Play Module
#10

Cadences

Cadences are musical punctuation. Learn authentic (V-I), plagal (IV-I), half (ending on V), and deceptive (V-vi) cadences.

🎵 100 XP ▶ Play Module
#11

Circle of Fifths

The circle of fifths organizes all 12 keys by perfect fifths. Moving clockwise adds sharps, counterclockwise adds flats. Essential for understanding key relationships.

🎵 75 XP ▶ Play Module
#12

Dominant Seventh Chord

The V7 chord adds tension with its tritone between the 3rd and 7th. It strongly resolves to I. Learn to build and resolve V7 chords.

🎵 100 XP ▶ Play Module
3

Counterpoint

#13

First Species Counterpoint

Note against note. Write a counterpoint melody above a given cantus firmus using only consonant intervals (unison, 3rd, 5th, 6th, octave). No dissonances allowed.

🎵 125 XP ▶ Play Module
#14

Second Species Counterpoint

Two notes against one. Write two notes in the counterpoint for each note of the cantus firmus. The first note of each pair must be consonant; the second may be a passing dissonance.

🎵 125 XP ▶ Play Module
#15

Third Species Counterpoint

Four notes against one. The counterpoint moves in quarter notes against whole notes in the cantus firmus. Learn neighbor tones, passing tones, and the cambiata figure.

🎵 150 XP ▶ Play Module
#16

Fourth Species Counterpoint

Syncopations. The counterpoint is tied across the barline, creating suspensions. Learn the suspension chain: preparation → suspension (dissonance) → resolution.

🎵 150 XP ▶ Play Module
4

Musical Forms

#17

Binary Form

Structure your music in two contrasting sections (A and B). Each section is usually repeated. The A section establishes the tonic; B section contrasts and returns home.

🎵 150 XP ▶ Play Module
#18

Ternary Form (ABA)

The most elegant form: A (statement) → B (contrast) → A (return). The return of A after contrast creates a satisfying sense of homecoming. Used in minuets, scherzos, and countless songs.

🎵 150 XP ▶ Play Module
#19

Rondo Form

A recurring theme (A) alternates with contrasting episodes (B, C, etc.). Common patterns: ABACA, ABACABA. The refrain creates familiarity while episodes provide variety.

🎵 175 XP ▶ Play Module
#20

Theme and Variations

Present a theme, then transform it through melody, rhythm, harmony, or texture while keeping it recognizable. Each variation reveals new facets of the original idea.

🎵 175 XP ▶ Play Module
#21

Sonata Form — Exposition

The crowning achievement of Classical form. The exposition presents two contrasting themes (theme 1 in tonic, theme 2 in dominant/relative major), connected by a transition.

🎵 200 XP ▶ Play Module
5

Advanced Harmony

#22

Modulation to Closely Related Keys

Modulate smoothly to keys that differ by at most one accidental. Use pivot chords — chords shared between both keys — to create seamless key changes.

🎵 175 XP ▶ Play Module
#23

Altered Chords and Augmented Sixths

Explore chromatic harmony with borrowed chords, Neapolitan sixth, and augmented sixth chords (Italian, French, German). These add rich color and dramatic tension.

🎵 200 XP ▶ Play Module
#24

Ninth, Eleventh, and Thirteenth Chords

Extend harmony beyond the seventh. Ninth, eleventh, and thirteenth chords add color and complexity, essential in jazz, impressionism, and modern composition.

🎵 200 XP ▶ Play Module
#25

Chromaticism

Use chromatic passing tones, chromatic neighbors, and chromatic harmonies to create smooth, colorful lines. Chromaticism blurs the boundaries of diatonic harmony.

🎵 175 XP ▶ Play Module
#26

Modal Interchange

Borrow chords from parallel modes. Use chords from C minor while in C major (or vice versa) to create unexpected colors and emotional shifts.

🎵 175 XP ▶ Play Module

Orchestration & Styles

#27

Instrumental Ranges

Every instrument has a playable range and a 'sweet spot.' Learn the ranges of strings, woodwinds, brass, and voice to write idiomatically for each.

🎵 150 XP ▶ Play Module
#28

Writing for Strings

The string section is the heart of the orchestra. Learn bowing techniques, double stops, pizzicato, and how to write effective string quartets.

🎵 200 XP ▶ Play Module
#29

Writing for Winds

Woodwinds and brass each have unique capabilities and limitations. Learn to write for flute, oboe, clarinet, bassoon, horn, trumpet, and trombone.

🎵 175 XP ▶ Play Module
#30

Baroque Style

Compose in Baroque style (1600-1750). Continuous rhythmic drive, terraced dynamics, ornamentation, basso continuo, and contrapuntal textures.

🎵 200 XP ▶ Play Module
#31

Classical Style

Compose in Classical style (1750-1820). Clear phrase structures (4+4 bars), Alberti bass, balanced periods, homophonic texture with elegant melody.

🎵 200 XP ▶ Play Module
#32

Romantic Style

Compose in Romantic style (1820-1900). Expressive chromatic harmony, rubato, expanded forms, programmatic content, and emotional intensity.

🎵 225 XP ▶ Play Module
#33

Impressionist Style

Compose in Impressionist style (Debussy, Ravel). Whole-tone scales, pentatonic melodies, planing, unresolved 7ths and 9ths, parallel chords, and color over function.

🎵 225 XP ▶ Play Module
#34

Contemporary Style

Compose using 20th/21st-century techniques. Polyrhythms, extended techniques, non-traditional notation, prepared piano, electronics, and mixed media concepts.

🎵 225 XP ▶ Play Module

Advanced Composition

#35

Polytonality

Use two or more keys simultaneously. Each hand can be in a different key, or different instruments play in different tonalities, creating rich harmonic tension.

🎵 250 XP ▶ Play Module
#36

Atonality and Twelve-Tone Technique

Liberate composition from tonal hierarchy. Use all 12 chromatic notes equally. Learn the twelve-tone row technique (prime, retrograde, inversion, retrograde inversion).

🎵 250 XP ▶ Play Module
#37

Serialism

Extend the twelve-tone principle beyond pitch: serialize rhythm, dynamics, articulation, and timbre. Total serialism treats all musical parameters as organized series.

🎵 275 XP ▶ Play Module
#38

Minimalism

Compose using minimal means: repetitive patterns, gradual transformation (phasing, additive process), pulse-based rhythm, and harmonic stasis. Less becomes more.

🎵 225 XP ▶ Play Module
#39

Final Project — Free Composition

Your capstone. Compose an original piece of at least 32 measures using everything you've learned. Choose your style, form, and instrumentation. There are no wrong answers — only your voice.

🎵 500 XP ▶ Play Module
